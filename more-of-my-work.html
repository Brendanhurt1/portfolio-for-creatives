In my freetime, I love to create movie posters for some of my favourite films. The art of illustrators like Bob Peak and Drew Struzan are a major inspiration for the kind of movie posters I love.

I started falling in love with the bold colours you can get by combining alcohol markers with pencil crayons, and since then has been my main medium for illustrations I do in my free time.

Often, I take inspiration from pop culture figures, but I also love doing architecture pieces to expand on different skills, blending a multitude of colours together.

I love illustration and have done a few commissions. When it comes to branding design, I can work quickly without getting hung up on small details. However, when it comes to illustration, my process is thorough and often takes time.

I devised a hypothetical French Canadian beer with a campaign aimed at uniting both English and French Canadians. I created multiple slogans for a truly Canadian beer that encourages Canadians to put their differences aside and have a beer so good that neither of them can disagree with its flavour.

I wanted them to be lighthearted and immedietly grab the attention of beer patrons throughout all of Canada. It was also a great exercise in simple layout design.

I designed a hypothetical magazine as an exercise in layout design, combining illustration, photography and long-form text. The magazine details film and music intertwining from a specific decade, in this case, the 1970s in New York. By combining the aesthetics of the topic while maintaining a consistent style across, a seamless magazine is created.
